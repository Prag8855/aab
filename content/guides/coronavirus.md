## Latest restrictions

### Changes on February 2

You don't need to wear a mask on public transit[^0].

### Before February 2

Since April, there are only two restrictions:

- You must wear an FFP2 mask on public transit.
- You must wear an FFP2 mask and show a negative test to visit hospitals and nursing homes.

**The full list of restrictions is [on Berlin.de](https://www.berlin.de/corona/en/measures/)**. For the latest news, follow the [press releases from the mayor](https://www.berlin.de/rbmskzl/aktuelles/pressemitteilungen/).

## Where to ask questions

If you have coronavirus symptoms, or questions about getting tested, call one of the coronavirus hotlines.

- **[Coronavirus hotline for all of Berlin](https://www.berlin.de/corona/en/hotline/)**[  
    030 / 9028 2828](tel:+493090282828)  
    The phone menu is in German, but they speak English[^1]. They can only give you information. They can't give you a test or vaccine appointment.
- **Vaccine hotline for all of Berlin**[  
    030 / 9028 2200](tel:+493090282200)
- **[Medical call service](https://www.116117.de/)** (*Ã¤rztliche Bereitschaftsdienst*)[  
    116 117](tel:+49116117), or [+49 116 117](tel:+49116117)  
    They can answer your questions, and help you get tested[^2]. They also have a [coronavirus test appointment service](https://eterminservice.de/terminservice) on their website. The phone menu is in German.s
- **Emergency services**  
    If you have serious symptoms, and you need medical help, call the emergency number: [112](tel:112).
