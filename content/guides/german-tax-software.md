This guide lists software that can help you file a tax declaration in Germany. It also shows alternatives to tax software.

Tax and bookkeeping software costs are [tax-deductible](/glossary/steuerlich%20absetzbar).[^0]

## List of tax software

### Tax software for employees

If you don't speak German, try these English-speaking options:

- [Wundertax](/out/wundertax)
- [Taxfix](/out/taxfix)
- [SteuerGo](/out/steuergo)

You can also file your taxes yourself in [[ELSTER]]. It's free, but it's only in German.

### Tax software for freelancers

If you don't speak German, try these English-speaking options:

- [Accountable](/out/accountable)
- [Sorted](/out/sorted-taxes)

Some business banks help you with your bookkeeping, invoicing, and [VAT reporting](/glossary/Umsatzsteuervoranmeldung):

- [Holvi](/out/holvi)
- [Kontist](/out/kontist) (read my [review of Kontist](/guides/kontist-review) first)
- [Qonto](/out/qonto)

Personally, I use [Lexoffice](/out/lexoffice), but it's only in German.

## Alternatives to tax software

- [Hire a tax advisor](/guides/english-speaking-steuerberater-berlin). Use my list of English-speaking tax advisors.
- Join a *[[Lohnsteuerhilfeverein]]*.
- Some business banks include basic bookkeeping tools. For example, [Holvi](/out/holvi), [Kontist](/out/kontist) and [Qonto](/out/qonto) have automatic [VAT reporting](/glossary/Umsatzsteuervoranmeldung).
