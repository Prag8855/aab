---
Title: How to open a German bank account without an Anmeldung
Short_title: How to get a bank account with no Anmeldung
Description: These German banks let you open an account without an official address in Germany. No Meldebestätigung or a Meldebescheinigung needed!
Date_created: 2018-07-31
---

When you move to Germany, you need a bank account. These banks let you open an account **without a registered address in Germany**. You don't need to do your *[[Anmeldung]]*, and you don't need a registration certificate (*[[Anmeldebestätigung]]*). This is useful if you just moved to Germany.

## Banks that don't require an [[Anmeldung]]

These banks will let you open an account without a registered address in Germany:

- **[bunq](/out/bunq)**
- **[Commerzbank](/out/commerzbank)**  
    They require a [residence permit](/glossary/Aufenthaltstitel).
- **[Deutsche Bank](/out/deutsche-bank)**
- **[DKB](/out/dkb)**[^0]  
    They rarely accept people who just moved to Germany. They use WebID to verify your passport.
- **[ING](/out/ing)**[^6]  
    They use [PostIdent](/glossary/Postident) to verify your passport.[^1] They require a EU citizenship or [permanent residence](/glossary/Niederlassungserlaubnis).[^2]
- **[Kontist](/out/kontist)**  
    Business bank for freelancers.
- **[Monese](/out/monese)**  
    They are based in the UK and in Belgium. Your bank account will have a Belgian [[IBAN]].
- **[N26](/out/n26)**  
    They sometimes require a residence permit. It depends on what passport you have.
- **[Revolut](/out/revolut)**
- **[Wise](/out/wise-account)**  
    They are based in the UK and in Belgium.[^3] Your bank account will have a Belgian [[IBAN]].

**[How to choose a German bank ➞](/guides/first-bank-account-in-germany)**

## Banks that require an [[Anmeldung]]

These banks require a registration certificate ([*Anmeldebestätigung*](/glossary/Anmeldebest%C3%A4tigung)) to open an account:

- [Berliner Sparkasse](/out/sparkasse)[^4]
- [Comdirect](/out/comdirect)[^5]

## Other problems you might have

If you are an immigrant, it can be hard to open a bank account in Germany:

- Some banks don't accept your foreign passport.
- Some banks require a [residence permit](/glossary/Aufenthaltstitel) or [permanent residence](/glossary/Niederlassungserlaubnis).
- If you don't speak German, you might want an English-speaking bank.
