A Schengen visa (also called a tourist visa or Category C visa[^0]) allows you to visit Germany and other [Schengen countries](https://www.axa-schengen.com/en/countries-schengen-area) for up to 90 days. You can't work or study in Germany with a Schengen visa.[^5]

You can rarely convert a Schengen visa to a [residence permit](/glossary/Aufenthaltstitel) like the [work visa](/guides/german-work-visa), Blue Card, student visa or [freelance visa](/guides/how-to-get-a-german-freelance-visa)[^1]. Normally, you must leave Germany, and apply for a German residence permit in your home country. You can rarely extend a Schengen visa[^2].

If you are a citizen of Australia, Canada, Israel, Japan, New Zealand, South Korea, the United Kingdom and the United States, you don't need a Schengen visa. You can visit Germany and [Schengen countries](https://www.schengenvisainfo.com/schengen-visa-countries-list/) for up to 90 days without a visa[^3]. If you want to stay longer, you can apply for a [residence permit](/glossary/Aufenthaltstitel) in your home country, or while you are in Germany.

Use [this list](https://www.auswaertiges-amt.de/en/einreiseundaufenthalt/-/231148) to know if you need a Schengen visa to visit Germany.

If you are a citizen of the European Union, Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway or Switzerland, you don't need a visa to visit Germany. You can live, work and study in Germany without a visa[^4].
