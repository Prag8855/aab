What does this mean?

When you worked in Germany, you put around 9.3% of your salary into the German pension system. You might be able to get that money back.

After you leave Germany, you must wait two years to request a refund. Many people forget, so they never get their money back. This is why you set a reminder on All About Berlin.

To read more about it, visit https://allaboutberlin.com/guides/pension-payments-refund. If you have questions, you can also reply to this email.

How much money will I get back?

Around 9.3% of your income. The more you earned, the more you will get back. The average refund is around 7,000€, but some people get more 30,000€ back.

How do I get my money back?

You must make a request to the Deutsche Rentenversicherung. This takes around 2 months, sometimes longer.
